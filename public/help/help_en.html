Tool Bar

Group of buttons
Zoom out to full map

This button restores the map to its original size.

Previous Image

This button passes to the preceding map zoom image.

Next Image

This button passes to the successive map zoom image.

Map Scale

The map scale indicates the scale at which the map will be visualised; clicking on the right arrow opens a window with a list of predefined scales for imposing a different scale on the map.

Zoom in

This passes to a smaller scale with greater detail. It has two modes:

A) SIMPLE ENLARGEMENT:

  1. Select the button desired
  2. Left click the mouse on the point to be enlarged. An enlargement twice the size of the original will appear and the point selected will correspond to the centre of the new map.

B) SELECTION OF AN AREA:

  1. Select the button desired
  2. Left click the mouse on the map and, keeping the left key pressed down, draw a rectangle around the area to be enlarged
  3. Release the mouse and the map will be created with the zoom on the area selected

Zoom out

This passes to a larger scale. Thereafter follow the same steps as for enlargement.

Sposta

Moves the map

  1. Select the button desired
  2. 2. Move the map to the desired new position by scrolling the mouse across the map keeping the left key pressed down. Release the mouse to fix the new position.


Group of buttons
Select the button desired

Shows the information of the “Selection Group” imposed.

  1. Select the button desired
  2. Left click the mouse on the map button on which you want information
    OR
    Left click the mouse and, keeping the left key pressed down, select a rectangular area;
  3. A window will open with the results of the research.

Polygon selection

Shows the information of the “Selection Group” imposed.

  1. Select the button desired
  2. Draw a polygon by clicking the left mouse on the map, each click corresponds to the vertex of the polygon. To terminate the operation double left click the mouse; The surface area and perimeter of the chosen area will be shown below the map.
  3. A window will open with the results of the research.

Circle selection

Shows the information of the “Selection Group” imposed.

  1. Select the button desired
  2. Left click the mouse on the centre of the circle desired and then move the mouse to define the radius of your selection. To terminate the operation double left click the mouse; The surface area and radius of the chosen area will be shown below the map.
  3. A window will open with the results of the research.

Area selection

Shows the information of the “Selection Group” imposed.

  1. Select the button desired
  2. Left click the mouse on the centre of the area desired and then move the mouse to define the radius of your selection. To terminate the operation double left click the mouse; The surface area and radius of the chosen area will be shown below the map.
  3. A window will open with the results of the research.


Length

Allows the measurement of the length in m.

  1. Select the button desired
  2. Left click the mouse on one end of the polyline or segment to be measured. Move the cursor and left click the mouse on the other end of the segment or the vertex of the polyline. At each click the partial and total distance will appear under the map.

Area

Allows the measurement of the area in m².

  1. Select the button desired
  2. Left click the mouse on one end of the polyline or segment to be measured. Move the cursor and left click the mouse on the other end of the segment or the vertex of the polyline. At each click the surface area and perimeter will appear on the left below the map.

Map Relaod

Clicking on this button regenerates the map while maintaining the zoom.

Top
Printing

Creates a map on the same scale as that visualised with the possibility of including a legend. It is possible to configure the following printing options:

  • Printing scale imposed manually (automatic prompt of the map scale)
  • Vertical or horizontal orientation
  • A4 (21cmX27,9m) and A3 (42cmX27,9cm) formats
  • Printing of legend
The result is a PDF file with an extract of the map. It is also possible to download the map image actually visualised in PNG or GeoTIFF at 150 or 300 dpi.

Image download

Enables map downloading in PNG or GeoTIFF at a resolution of 150 or 300 dpi.

Reload Reference

Creates a new reference image of the same size and with the same elements as the map visualised.

Options

Configuration of the interrogation parameters.

Levels

The themes and levels that can be visualised on the map are shown on the right of the map. It is possibile to visualise or hide the the theme with a tick.

Legend

The legend shows all the themes and levels visualisable on the map at its current scale with their respective colours and symbols. A deselected level does not appear in the legend.
